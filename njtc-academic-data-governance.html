Academic Data Collection and Reporting

üìä Mission Statement

We rigorously collect and analyze academic data to demonstrate the tangible impact of our high-impact tutoring program. This data provides concrete evidence ("FACTS") of scholar progress, particularly for those scholars who are targeted based on our mission (those performing 2 or more grade levels below).

Purpose & Impact

By showcasing this progress, we can demonstrate to stakeholders, potential funders, and partners that our targeted intervention is effective in helping the scholars who need it most.

Reporting and Governance

This collected data is not just an external report; we internalize the findings to understand both the success we have achieved and where further "learning is to be done."

üîÑ Multi-Year Progress Philosophy

For scholars who participate in multiple cycles, it's important to remember that for those working 2 or more grade levels below proficiency, the immediate target isn't grade-level proficiency itself, but consistent, steady progress toward it. As scholars get closer to grade level proficiency, the measurable gap gains should become incrementally smaller each year. This is a positive sign, indicating that over time, scholars are progressing and will eventually move up and out of NJTC Tutoring Programs.

iReady 5-Level Placement Definitions

üìã Overall Placement Legend

The 5-Level Placements provide greater insight into student performance using the same five placements that calculate student growth in the i-Ready growth model.

Mid or Above Grade Level
Early On Grade Level
One Grade Level Below
Two Grade Levels Below
Three or More Grade Levels Below

üü¢ Mid or Above Grade Level

Definition: Students at this level have met or surpassed the minimum requirements for the expectations of college- and career-ready standards in their grade level.

Implications for Instruction: Students will benefit from instruction in late on-grade level topics or above-grade level instruction.

  • Mid Grade Level: Benefit from late on-grade level topics
  • Late Grade Level: Ready for topics typically covered in the beginning of the subsequent grade level
  • Above Grade Level: Benefit from above-grade level instruction

Implications for Intervention: Students likely do not need specialized intervention.

üü¢ Early On Grade Level

Definition: Students at this level have partially met grade-level expectations. They will benefit from continued on-grade level instruction.

Implications for Instruction: These students will benefit from on-grade level instruction to help them meet the expectations of college- and career-ready standards for their grade level.

Implications for Intervention: Students likely do not need specialized intervention.

üü° One Grade Level Below

Definition: Students at this level are approaching grade-level expectations. They will benefit from continued on-grade level instruction.

Implications for Instruction:

  • Beginning-of-year Diagnostic: Performing consistently with students who have just begun their academic year
  • Midyear or end-of-year Diagnostic: May need intensive intervention

Implications for Intervention: Students may benefit from review or remediation of material that is one grade level below.

üü† Two Grade Levels Below

Definition: Students at this level will likely need additional support with key skills below their chronological grade level to be ready for grade-level instruction.

Implications for Instruction: These students may need intensive intervention.

Implications for Intervention: Remediation focused on material that is two grade levels below is recommended to help fill in gaps in students' foundational knowledge.

üî¥ Three or More Grade Levels Below

Definition: Students at this level will likely need additional support with key skills below their chronological grade level to be ready for grade-level instruction.

Implications for Instruction: These students likely need intensive intervention.

Implications for Intervention: Intensive intervention of foundational concepts is recommended.

Are the 5-Level Placements Available at the Domain Level?
Yes! The 5-Level Placements are available at both the overall and domain level for all reports and exports. The teacher dashboard only features the 5-Level Placements for overall scores, but teachers can navigate to other reports to view the 5-Level Placements at the domain level.

Do Demographic Filters Work with the 5-Level Placements?
Yes, the 5-Level Placements are available with demographic filters for accounts with these filters enabled.
